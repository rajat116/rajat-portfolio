About Me

My Journey

I began my academic journey in Physics, completing my undergraduate and master’s degrees before earning a PhD in Particle Physics, where I conducted research at some of the world’s leading laboratories including CERN. My work involved analyzing large-scale experimental data, designing real-time algorithms, and applying machine learning techniques to uncover subtle patterns in complex environments.

During my postdoctoral research at the University of Pittsburgh and CERN, I deepened my expertise in scientific computing and AI-driven workflows. This evolved into a broader focus on data science and machine learning engineering, where I now specialize in building robust, end-to-end systems — from data ingestion to modeling and deployment — grounded in strong analytical thinking and driven by real-world impact.
